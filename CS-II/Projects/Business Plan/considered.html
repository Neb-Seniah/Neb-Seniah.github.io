TV360
  While an interesting idea, the TV360 has very little practical use. The problem is almost non-existent in most houses, and there are already mounts that can rotate a TV at 140 degree angles for patios. Also, this project doesn’t seem to be achievable over the course of a school year, as physical objects need to be developed alongside the code.

Smartshop
  This product also represents a pretty niche problem, while everyone should look presentable, a filter for these products already exists. The system used to track current trends is the development team, which is pretty inefficient as they need to spend their time looking into the current trends. Often, people go shopping just for the experience and ambience, it’s one of the reasons why malls still gain traction even though sites like Amazon exist to do shopping. Finally, there is no mention of selling user information, which is unrealistic.

The Cafe Project
  In our cafeteria, at least, times aren’t a problem, the food will pretty much be ready instantly, and you are still going to have to wait in lines to pick up your food, people can still cut, and things are going to be the same (except people might wait in line for nothing if there are any problems with the service). Additionally, this system introduces a new problem, the 25 cent fee, which is not negligible when people are spending around $4 on lunch (around $45 over the course of the year). Providing the bagging of food to Culinary Arts students sounds like a good idea, but what does it have to do with the actual Culinary Arts? You are cooking the pizza, not making something from scratch, also, where will they find the time? They have class during lunch periods and have to eat as well. Also, the data collection method used is shoddy at best, with only 11 people in the school giving their thoughts. At the end of the day, school lunch lines are a strategy game, and I just eat before the lunch period begins.

  On Track is an app that helps you plan trips to National Parks. The app allows you to set paths and navigates you into the quickest path that is the most fun as well. This immediately poses two problems, how far is the app willing to go for fun, will it rank the attractions in the National Park and send you to the most fun of them? Also, part of the fun of national parks is exploring it for yourself and seeing everything by yourself. If you are setting a strict path to follow then you are probably going somewhere in a hurry, in which case you probably wouldn’t want to go on detours without really knowing.
